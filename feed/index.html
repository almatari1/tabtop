]]> Have you ever heard of edge AI or edge intelligence? AI performs calculations directly on devices at the edge of the network. This approach does not rely on cloud-based or centralized data centers. This approach offers several benefits.

 Faster Response: This processes data locally, enabling real-time or near-real-time responses. Thus, this is crucial for applications like augmented reality, industrial automation, and autonomous vehicles.

Enhanced Security: Sensitive data remains local and doesn’t need to be sent to the cloud. Also reduces the risk of data breaches, and improves privacy. So, the data remains secure on the device.

Lower Bandwidth Usage: Edge AI transmits less data to and from the cloud. This leads to less network traffic and cost savings on bandwidth.

Improved Reliability: Systems with local processing can continue to operate even if the internet connection is unreliable or unavailable. This ensures uninterrupted functionality.
Scalability:This allows AI features to be implemented on multiple devices without overloading central servers. Additionally, it distributes the workload efficiently without additional strain.

So, Edge AI is all almost making things speedier, more private, and solid, whereas moreover being adaptable. It’s a win-win situation!

Understanding URL Whitelisting

URL whitelisting, also known as URL filtering, is a critical component of web access management. Thus, Sophos XG Firewall by creating a whitelist of approved URLs, administrators can ensure that users have access to trusted sites without compromising network security. This approach allows businesses to strike the right balance between productivity and security, enabling employees to access the resources they need while mitigating the risk of cyber threats.

How to Whitelist URLs with Sophos XG Firewall

You can easily whitelist URLs with Sophos XG Firewall through the firewall’s intuitive web interface. Here’s a step-by-step guide:

  1. Access the Dashboard: Log in to the Sophos XG Firewall web interface using your administrator credentials.
  2. Navigate to Web Filtering Policies: Locate the section where you can manage web filtering policies. This is typically found under the “Firewall” or “Protection” menu.
  3. Create or Modify a Policy: Create a new web filtering policy specifically for whitelisting URLs or modify an existing one.
  4. Add URLs to the Whitelist: Within the policy configuration, specify the URLs or domains that you want to whitelist. Ensure that you enter the URLs in the correct format, starting with “http://” or “https://”.
  5. Apply the Changes: Save the changes to the web filtering policy.
  6. Review and Test: Review the configuration to ensure that you have correctly whitelisted the URLs. Test access to the whitelisted URLs from devices behind the firewall to confirm accessibility.
  7. Monitor and Adjust: Regularly monitor Sophos XG Firewall logs and user feedback to ensure that the whitelisted URLs serve their intended purpose. Adjust the whitelist as needed based on changing requirements or user feedback.
